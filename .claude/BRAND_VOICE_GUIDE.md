# Dualys Brand Voice Guide

## Core Voice Attributes

### Analytical
- Data-driven statements
- Evidence-based claims
- Logical progression of ideas
- Quantifiable where possible

### Rigorous
- Precise terminology
- Thorough explanations
- Attention to technical detail
- No oversimplification

### Accessible
- Clear without being simplistic
- Professional without jargon overload
- Explanations for technical terms
- Welcoming to all audiences

## Tone Guidelines

### DO
- Use active voice: "We develop" not "Technologies are developed"
- Be direct: "Our solution detects threats" not "Threats can be detected"
- Show confidence: "We deliver" not "We try to deliver"
- Emphasize protection: "Safeguarding", "Securing", "Protecting"
- Focus on preparation: "Readiness", "Preparedness", "Resilience"
- Highlight deterrence: "Dissuasion", "Prevention", "Deterrence"

### DON'T
- Use aggressive military language: No "attack", "destroy", "eliminate"
- Be nationalistic: Avoid single-country focus
- Oversimplify: Don't dumb down technical content
- Be casual: Maintain professional register
- Make unsubstantiated claims: Always have backing

## Audience Adaptation

### Institutional (EU/Government)
- **Register**: Formal, compliance-aware
- **Focus**: Programs, standards, regulations
- **Keywords**: European, strategic autonomy, EDIDP, EDF, PESCO
- **Trust signals**: Certifications, partnerships, track record

### Industrial (Defense Contractors)
- **Register**: Technical, capability-focused
- **Focus**: Integration, reliability, performance
- **Keywords**: TRL levels, specifications, supply chain
- **Trust signals**: Case studies, technical documentation

### Academic (Research Centers)
- **Register**: Scholarly, collaborative
- **Focus**: Innovation, research, methodology
- **Keywords**: R&D, publications, patents, collaboration
- **Trust signals**: Research partnerships, publications

### Public (Citizens)
- **Register**: Accessible, values-driven
- **Focus**: Protection, democracy, safety
- **Keywords**: Preparedness, civil applications, firefighting, emergencies
- **Trust signals**: Social benefit, ethical approach

## Writing Examples

### Headlines

**Good**
- "Protecting Critical Infrastructure with Advanced Cyber Defense"
- "Enabling European Defense Autonomy Through Innovation"
- "Dual-Use Technologies: From Emergency Response to National Security"

**Avoid**
- "Superior Weapons for Maximum Impact"
- "Crushing Threats with Military Precision"
- "Spain's Path to Military Dominance"

### Body Copy

**Good**
> Dualys integrates cutting-edge sensor technologies with autonomous platforms, providing defense forces with enhanced situational awareness while enabling civilian emergency services to respond more effectively to natural disasters.

**Avoid**
> Our military-grade weapons systems deliver devastating firepower that will eliminate any threat to national security.

## Translation Notes

When translating content:

1. **Preserve tone**: Maintain professional register across languages
2. **Localize terminology**: Use region-appropriate defense/security terms
3. **Keep key messages**: Core positioning must remain consistent
4. **Adapt cultural context**: EU perspective in all languages

### Language-Specific Considerations (4 languages)

- **Catalan (ca)**: Default language. Emphasize local identity, Catalan ecosystem, European context. IEC normative standard. Elisions: l'empresa, d'innovació.
- **Spanish (es)**: Primary content language. Copy from briefing is source of truth. "Usted" in body, "tú" in CTAs. Spain Spanish (not LatAm).
- **English (en)**: International B2B professional. "Defense" (not "defence"). Adapt concepts, don't translate literally.
- **French (fr)**: Formal register, vouvoiement. EU partnership focus. "Technologies à double usage", "secteur de la défense".

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing any content:

- [ ] Does the copy match the briefing EXACTLY (for ES)?
- [ ] Does it align with the new positioning (consultora estratégica, not tech company)?
- [ ] Is the tone professional, strategic, and NOT militaristic?
- [ ] Are technical terms explained when needed?
- [ ] Does it emphasize European perspective?
- [ ] Are claims substantiated with real data (800.000 M€, 44.000 M€, 2% PIB)?
- [ ] Does it avoid forbidden terminology?
- [ ] Is it consistent across all 4 languages?
- [ ] Are CTAs using the exact text from the briefing?
- [ ] Are trust elements real (not invented like "ISO 27001 certified")?
